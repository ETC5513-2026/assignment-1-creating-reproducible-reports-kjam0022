Marketing Campaign Analysis

Author

Krrish Jambhulkar

Introduction

As the Head of Marketing, I reviewed the recent campaign to assess its impact on customer purchasing behaviour. This analysis was conducted to ensure our budget is being allocated effectively across different business segments. We evaluated the relationship between marketing spend and customer purchases to identify the most profitable strategies. These insights will guide our future campaigns by focusing on the customer behaviours that drive the best results.

Research Question

Did increased marketing spend encourage customers to make more purchases? We will analyze the patterns to determine which segments responded best to our campaign. Based on these insights, we can make informed decisions about allocating our future marketing budget.

Dataset Introduction

This dataset contains information on 150 customers from a marketing campaign conducted last quarter. For each customer, we have tracked their total spending and the number of visits, along with the marketing spend allocated per customer. Customers are also categorised into three segments: Consumer, SMB, and Enterprise.

Before analysis, data cleaning was performed to ensure consistency and accuracy. Date values were in multiple formats, so they were standardised into a single format. Customers who neither visited nor made any purchases were removed, as they did not contribute to the analysis, and the notes column was dropped due to its lack of relevant information.

Dataset Description

After cleaning, the dataset contains 122 observations and 6 variables. The variables include customer ID, signup date, marketing spend, visits, purchase amount, and segment, which are a mix of numeric and categorical variables.
